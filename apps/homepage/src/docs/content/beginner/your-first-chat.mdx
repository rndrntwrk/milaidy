# Your first chat

You've finished onboarding and you're staring at the chat view. Time to send something.

**What you'll learn:** how to send a message, what happens when you do, how voice works, and where your conversation actually lives.

## Say hi

Click the chat input at the bottom of the window and type something. A good first message is anything you'd say to a new assistant:

> "Hey — what can you do?"
>
> "Tell me about yourself."
>
> "What's the weather like?" *(spoiler: it probably doesn't know the weather yet)*

Hit Enter. A few things happen in the next couple of seconds:

1. Your message gets stored locally in the conversation.
2. The character's avatar reacts — a subtle animation, maybe a head turn.
3. Milady sends your message plus some context (who your agent is, recent conversation history) to whichever language model provider you picked.
4. The response streams back. You'll see text appearing word by word.
5. If voice is enabled, the character speaks the response out loud while the text renders.
6. The whole exchange gets saved to your local database.

That's the round trip. Do it a few times to get a feel for how your agent responds.

## Turn voice on (or off)

By default, voice is on — Milady speaks responses aloud using the voice you picked during onboarding. If you'd rather read in silence, there's a speaker icon near the chat that toggles it.

<Callout kind="tip">
Headphones are strongly recommended the first time you hear it. The default voices are good, and hearing an AI talk back through laptop speakers in a quiet room is a weirder experience than you might expect.
</Callout>

If voice is on but you don't hear anything, check:

- Your system audio output is pointed at the right device.
- The voice toggle is actually on (icon isn't crossed out).
- You're not in Do Not Disturb / focus mode that silences app audio.

## Talk mode

Want to have a hands-free conversation? Open the chat, click the microphone, and talk. Milady transcribes what you say, sends it through the model, and speaks the response back. This is "talk mode."

Talk mode uses your system microphone and Milady's speech-to-text pipeline. First time you enable it, your OS will ask for microphone permission — grant it. If you skip the prompt, you can re-enable it later in your system privacy settings (macOS: System Settings → Privacy & Security → Microphone; Windows: Settings → Privacy → Microphone).

## Where does the conversation live?

Right on your machine, in a local database under `~/.milady/` (Windows: `%USERPROFILE%\.milady\`).

That means:

- You can close Milady and reopen it later — the conversation is still there.
- Your messages aren't synced to a Milady-owned cloud. There isn't one.
- If you're using a cloud language model (like OpenAI), the message gets sent to them *during inference*, but the transcript is stored with you, not them.
- Backups and migrations are your responsibility. If you want to move Milady to a new machine, copy the `~/.milady/` directory and you're done.

## Starting a new conversation

There's a "new chat" button near the conversation list. Click it to start fresh with a blank slate. Your previous conversations don't disappear — they're just shelved under the chat list. Click any of them to pick up where you left off.

<Callout kind="note">
Each conversation is independent. Your agent remembers things you told it in one conversation, but it won't automatically surface them in a new conversation unless you've set up memory / knowledge (we'll cover that in the Intermediate tier).
</Callout>

## Things to try before moving on

A few prompts that show off different capabilities:

- **Ask about itself.** "What model are you running?" "What character are you?" "Where does your memory live?"
- **Ask for help with something concrete.** "Help me write a short birthday message for a friend." Real tasks show you the tone and style best.
- **Ask it to remember something.** "Remember that my favorite color is green." Then start a new conversation and ask "what's my favorite color?" and see what happens. (Spoiler: nothing, yet — persistent memory across sessions requires a step you'll learn in the Intermediate tier.)
